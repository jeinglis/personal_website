Growing up in a family of medical professionals I had always planned on attending medical school. This plan landed me in Halifax pursuing a Neuroscience degree at Dalhousie University during which I realized I enjoyed problem solving but not necessarily the field of Medicine. Following the completion of my neuro degree I returned to Lethbridge where I worked as a bartender while I planned my next move. I soon found myself enrolled in Engineering at the University of Calgary. Originally planning to enter the Electrical Engineering department to complement my previous education. I found that I enjoyed my software classes far more than any of the others and decided to pursue software engineering instead. In 2016, following my third year of Eng, I obtained a 16 month internship with Transcanada Pipelines Ltd. which I enjoyed emmensely and not only further developed my software design, documentaion and development skills but I also learned the complexities of working in a large corporation. During my internship I got married to my best friend who I met during my time in Halifax. I am currently finishing my final year of engineering. and I couldn't be more excited to start the next chapter.

As part of the the Decision Support Systems (DSS) team at TransCanada Pipelines Ltd., I had the opportunity to learn many aspects of industrial software with a focus on SCADA and data historization. My primary duties as an engineering intern were centered around ensuring the integrity of our data and the interfaces we depended on to collect data from site. I was also seen as the support frontline, handling the majority of support tickets and escalating when I needed help. When I completed all of the tasks I was responsible for, the team supported my enthusiasm for software development and provided me with a wealth of development opportunities. As a result I designed, implemented and documented multiple programs - the largest of which collected a multitude of system and security information including general hardware information, shares, local groups, local accounts, logon events, USB device insertion, and more. By using remote calls I was able to collect all of this information for 16 of our servers from a central location and write it to a SQL db. I then developed a JSP web application to present the information in a consumable format. The web app analyzed the data and flagged unexpected logon instances, any new USB device activity or any changes to the server between user defined dates. A dashboard outlining server changes over the past week was emailed to the team every monday morning. This server monitoring utility was built to aid in server troubleshooting as well as to provide another level of security. Other software I developed included an email reader/parser, which monitored an exchange inbox for a specific type of email then grabbed the attachment, parsed it, and used a subset of the spreadsheet data for calculations, the processed data was then written to a SQL db for further processing. Finally I wrote a few C# libraries to expediate future development. These libraries were for parsing excel files of all types, querying and writing to SQL dbs, application logging and various exchange server tasks including finding emails, obtaining attachments and sending emails. I primarily worked with C#, java, html and CSS however I did utilize javascript, PowerShell and batch when necessary.

Working under the I.T. Manager for a group of agricultural companies, I was primarily responsible for computer technical support and maintenance. I also worked as part of a team to carry out a variety of I.T. initiatives including the redesign and development of multiple websites
